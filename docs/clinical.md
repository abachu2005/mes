# Clinical interpretation

!!! danger "Research use only"
    Do not use MES for clinical decision-making. This page is a guide for
    research staff interpreting demo output.

## Reading a score

| MES range | Suggested interpretation |
|---|---|
| 70 – 100 | Strong, lateralized motor engagement consistent with task-typical activation. |
| 40 – 70  | Moderate engagement. Some ERD and/or lateralization present but variable. |
| 20 – 40  | Weak engagement. Mu/beta drop minimal; lateralization small or absent. |
| 0 – 20   | Minimal engagement. Indistinguishable from rest. |

## What to look at besides MES

1. **Per-trial trace.** A stable score around the mean is more reassuring
   than a noisy one with large swings. Watch for increasing scores across
   the session — a possible sign of warming up.
2. **ERD topomap.** The "hot spot" should be over the contralateral motor
   cortex for unilateral tasks. Bilateral or ipsilateral activation suggests
   compensation or reorganization.
3. **Lateralization index.** Positive = contralateral dominance (expected for
   the target task). Negative LI in a unilateral task is unusual.
4. **MRCP amplitude.** Larger negative deflections typically reflect more
   intent — even when the muscle never fires.

## Longitudinal tracking

MES is intended to be looked at *over time*. A single session is just a
snapshot; the value comes from watching scores recover across weeks of
rehab. The frontend shows a per-participant longitudinal chart with
±1 SD error bars.
